The workshop is dedicated to bringing together distinguished scholars, researchers, and practitioners in the field of Natural Language Generation (NLG) to discuss and explore the challenges and opportunities in controlling content in NLG systems. The interplay between controlled-generation research and the instruction-learning paradigm is the primary focus of this workshop.

In today's rapidly evolving NLG landscape, where powerful generative models have the potential to revolutionize industries and replace a partial workforce across various job types, ensuring the controllability of these systems is of paramount importance. Controllable NLG is essential for practical applications, such as editing assistants, creative AI, and persona-based dialogue agents. It is also critical in addressing ethical concerns, like the generation of toxic, gender, and racial content.


Our workshop aims to create a platform for experts working on different models and tasks related to controllable text generation to share task-agnostic solutions and evaluations, as well as discuss task-specific challenges. By fostering communication, collaboration, and research among attendees, the workshop will facilitate insightful debates about the recent progress of the NLG community.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Mechanisms for controllable generation with the interaction of pretrained large language models.
  • Development of resources, datasets, and novel evaluation metrics for controllable NLG.
  • Efficiency in controlled generation methods.
  • Instruction-based control and comparison with other controlling mechanisms.
  • Generality in controllable text generation tasks and metrics.
  • Alignment of model objectives with human preferences.
  • Fairness and representation in language models.
  • Interpretability and transparency of NLG models.
  • Misuse potential and responsible deployment of AI systems.
  • Model exploration, including limitations, linguistic properties, commonsense reasoning, and potential applications.
  • Robustness and reliability of generative models under various conditions.

The CtrlNLG Workshop will be co-located with SIGDial 2023 and INLG 2023! We look forward to your participation in this exciting workshop!
