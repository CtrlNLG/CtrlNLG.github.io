Making output controllable is an important and fundamental issue in Natural Language Generation (NLG). For example, in real-world applications, the output of story generation systems is often expected to follow a given storyline. Moreover, for ethical development, it is crucial to avoid generating biased and offensive violations such as gender and racial discrimination. Therefore, if the controllability of an NLG system can not be guaranteed, it becomes hard to generate significant practical value in real-world applications.

The workshop focuses on the formulation and evaluation of various controllable generation tasks and their real applications. It will gather researchers as well as practitioners in the field for discussions, communicating ideas, and promoting research. It will also generate insightful debates about the recent progress of a broader community.


In this workshop, we would like to create a platform for people working on different models and tasks related to controllable text generation to share task-agnostic solutions and evaluations, as well as to discuss task-specific challenges. It will gather researchers as well as practitioners in the field for discussions, communicating ideas, and promoting research. It will also generate insightful debates about the recent progress of a broader community.

Topics of interests include but not limited to

  • Tasks and Resources : Designing challenging tasks and building datasets to evaluate modelsâ€™ generation being consistent with a set of predefined features; Resources that cover attributes that include stylistic features (such as sentiment, formality, and personality, etc.), factual consistency (such as world facts, keywords, and entities both from input or external knowledge, etc.), structural adherence (such as storylines, and event plot, etc.), and fair generation (covering aspects including gender, race, and toxicity). Improved resources and benchmarks in applications, such as dialogue generationn, storytelling, data-to-text, debiasing, and format control, particularly for multi-attribute control.
  • Methods : Methods for controllable text generation; methods that learn language models of specific linguistic attributes (e.g., GAN, reinforcement learning); methods that specialize general foundation models (e.g., fine-tuning, prompt/prefix tuning); methods that support lightweight and plug-and-play control (e.g., weighted decoding, parameter-efficient adapters); methods that improve the interpretability and explainability of generation processes; methods that mitigate different types of biases; methods that improve security and preserve privacy; methods that can continually adapt to new attributes of control; methods that are flexible for positive, negative, and tunable control; methods that are generic and applicable across tasks.
  • Evaluation: Evaluation schemas and metrics for controllable text generations. The evaluation aspects include measuring the control accuracy/strength or success rates, as well as the general quality of generated text (e.g., fluency, coherence, and informativeness) and factual faithfulness. We encourage the development of automatic, manual, and semi-automatic evaluation strategies. Particularly, for automatic metrics, topics include both reference-based metrics and reference-free metrics, learnable metrics, and heuristic metrics.
